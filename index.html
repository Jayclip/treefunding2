Trees to plant?

Live Oak

The live oak, which is an evergreen oak, is able to sequester the most carbon of all trees over its lifetime. This is partly due to its high leaf surface area, allowing it to photosynthesize at a much faster rate than other species. The more live oaks we plant, particularly in areas of deforestation or high carbon emissions, the less carbon will enter our atmosphere and contribute to climate breakdown.

Acacia

The acacia is a key tree in many African ecosystems. Acacias have complex roots which penetrate deep enough to reach the water table, so they help prevent desertification of grassland and provide a much needed source of water for essential grassland species. All of this means that areas with an abundance of acacia trees will support richer biodiversity and be more resilient to higher temperatures and lack of rainfall, increasing climate resilience.

Location

Dinagat island, Philippines

In Dinagat Islands, extensive mining activities have significantly altered the landscape, resulting in environmental degradation and the loss of critical habitats. The removal of vegetation has left the region susceptible to soil erosion, negatively impacting water quality and aquatic ecosystems. Replanting trees in Dinagat Islands is essential to address these issues.

Dinagat Islands faces deforestation and mining disruption, leading to soil erosion and sedimentation. Implementing tree planting initiatives can mitigate erosion, restore habitats, and improve water quality. Reintroducing native tree species creates a protective canopy, fostering a healthier environment for threatened species and restoring the island's natural balance.

Welcome to treefunding

At Treefunding, we are on a mission to create a greener, healthier planet. Since 2020, we have been dedicated to combating climate change, preserving ecosystems, and fostering sustainable communities through the simple act of planting trees.

Our Mission

Our mission is to reforest landscapes and combat climate change, focusing on the transformative power of trees. Each tree planted represents hope for a sustainable future, benefiting both the environment and communities. By offsetting carbon emissions and creating thriving ecosystems, we aim to ensure a healthier planet, cleaner air, and a sustainable future for generations to come.

The Power of Trees

Did you know one mature tree can sustain two humans for a year, and an acre can provide oxygen for 18 people. Tree species have medicinal properties, and extracts are used in medicine. Some trees can live for hundreds or thousands of years, leaving a lasting legacy for future generations. Planting trees today invests in our planet's well-being.
